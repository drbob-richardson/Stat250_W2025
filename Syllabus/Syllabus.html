Course Description

Introduce programming concepts essential for success as a statistician. These include

  • Basic to Advanced Programming Knowledge
  • Data Wrangling and Visualization
  • Simulation, sampling, and testing

Exams

Both the midterm and the final exam will have two parts:

  1. Part I (In-Class, AI-Free)
    • Taken in class under standard exam conditions.
    • No AI tools may be used.
    • Anything in your Stat 250 Project directory can be used, including course material and any notes you have made
  2. Part II (Take-Home, AI-Allowed)
    • After completing Part I, you will receive the same exam to redo at home.
    • You may use your notes, AI tools, Google, or other resources to check and improve your work.
    • The goal is for you to reflect on and learn from your initial attempt.

AI

I am in the camp of professors who think AI is amazing and we should be using it. This class is a great place for you to understand the limits of an AI tool, like ChatGPT, in helping you code. For example, debugging with ChatGPT is much easier than before. We will do a significant amount of coding in class, often in settings where you can work with other people. I would ask the following:

In Class

  1. First, look at the course material and notes.
  2. If you’re stuck, ask a neighbor, TA, or the instructor.
  3. Only after those steps, feel free to use AI or Google.
    • Use these tools not to get “the answer” directly, but to learn how you would solve the problem.

At Home

  1. First, look at the course notes and materials.
  2. If you’re still stuck, you may:
    • Ask Google or an AI tool, or
    • Ask a question on Discord.
      Both options are fine.

Exams

  • The midterm and final each have two parts:
    1. Part I: Taken in class, AI-free.
    2. Part II: A take-home re-take where you redo Part I and may use AI or other resources to check and improve your work.
